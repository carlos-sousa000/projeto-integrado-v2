Frege's Logic - Gottlob Frege

Frege's Logic was created by Friedrich Frege to remove ambiguity from sentences. He invented the use of variables (names that can represent any element of a set, e.g., person, mortal) and quantifiers (symbols that mean "for all" or "exists"). The symbols are ∀ (for all) and ∃ (exists). Example: ∀x (Human(x) → Mortal(x)) means "for all x, if x is human, then x is mortal".

Overview

Historical Context

Friedrich Ludwig Gottlob Frege was a mathematician and logician from Germany in the 19th century. His work helped create rules for modern logic. Part of his studies was lost in a bombing during World War II, but what remained helped shape modern logic.

How did it work?

It has variables, predicates that state a characteristic or relationship, and quantifiers, and with these, you can make any sentence unambiguous. Example: ∀x (Human(x) → Mortal(x)) = "All humans are mortal." ∃x (Human(x) ∧ mortal(x)) = "There exists a human who is mortal."
