Keyboard Calculator - Torchi

The Keyboard Calculator introduced a key-based input system that anticipated modern input devices and made a significant contribution to the history of technology and computing.

Overview In 1834, the Italian inventor Luigi Torchi created the first mechanical calculator with a keyboard, capable of performing basic operations such as addition, subtraction, multiplication and division. The use of keys to enter numbers was a notable innovation, making calculations faster and more practical.

Historical context

During the 19th century, many inventors sought to improve the calculating machines started by Pascal and Leibniz. In this environment of mechanical progress, Torchi presented in Milan a different machine that replaced cranks and wheels with a numeric keyboard, bringing mechanical calculation closer to human writing.

Contributions to history

Torchi's calculator was the first to use a functional keyboard, influencing the design of future calculating machines and, later, computer keyboards. Although his invention received little publicity, it represented an important step in the mechanization of calculation.

The invention's importance

Torchi's creation marked the transition between purely mechanical calculating machines and devices with direct user interaction, paving the way for modern input devices. His work helped establish the keyboard as an essential means of communication between people and machines.
