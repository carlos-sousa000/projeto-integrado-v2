Innovators of the Era

Babbage

Considered the father of the computer, Charles Babbage conceptualized the Analytical Engine, a forerunner to modern computers. His vision of a machine capable of performing automatic calculations paved the way for computer science.

Joseph Henry

American physicist and inventor, Joseph Henry was a pioneer in the studies of electromagnetism. His discoveries influenced the creation of the telegraph and spurred the development of electrical communication.

Ada Lovelace

Mathematician and writer, Ada Lovelace was the first to recognize that a machine could go beyond just numbers. She created the first algorithm for Babbage's Analytical Engine, being recognized as the first programmer in history.

Hollerith

Herman Hollerith revolutionized data processing by creating the tabulating machine based on punched cards. His system was essential for the 1890 United States census and gave rise to the future IBM.

Gottlob Frege

German philosopher and mathematician, Frege is considered the founder of modern mathematical logic. His "predicate calculus" formalized logical thought, establishing the essential theoretical foundations for computer science.

Alfred Smee

British surgeon and inventor, Smee proposed the "Relational Machine" in 1851. His theory described a mechanical device capable of imitating thought processes and associative memory, one of the first concepts linked to artificial intelligence.
